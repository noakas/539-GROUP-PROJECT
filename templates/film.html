Current Projects

Preservation concerns for digital content are vastly different than those for analog content. A photographic negative could be left untouched for years and still have the physical integrity to be viewed and manipulated. A digital image left on a hard-drive could cease to exist with the transient life of its home or file format. Dr. Francine Berman, a leader in digital data preservation, has described the concern of “developing, maintaining, and sustaining the infrastructure” necessary to preserve assets as the “Achilles’ heel” of data-driven research and innovation. Without sustaining an infrastructure that supports digital preservation — through community standards, technical components, and the oversight of information professionals — we risk complete loss of our assets.

Since starting the UMSI program I have focused my studies on digital image preservation. These studies have been complimented by several projects.
